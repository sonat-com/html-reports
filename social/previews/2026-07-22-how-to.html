Writing a help doc that actually deflects tickets comes down to 4 small choices:

1. Title it in the exact words your reader would use.
2. Put the answer in the first two lines — save the background for lower down.
3. Keep it to one task: numbered steps, one screenshot, nothing extra.
4. Add a "Was this helpful?" so real usage shows you what to fix next.

That last step is the one most teams skip — and it's what keeps a help center honest. More on building docs people actually finish: https://sonat.com

3. One task per article: numbered steps, one screenshot, nothing extra.

Write it once, and let the doc do the answering.
