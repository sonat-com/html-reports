Recommendations testable hypotheses

  1. Keep scaling in measured steps

    Evidence: a +59.7% spend increase produced +54.4% Link Clicks and +53.3% Impressions while Frequency stayed low (1.19) and Link Click CTR held (3.07%). No sign of audience fatigue at this pressure. Hypothesis: further 15–25% weekly budget steps should keep adding Link Clicks at near-current efficiency. Guardrails / expected impact on overall performance: watch Frequency and CPC (all) as you scale; if Frequency climbs well past ~2 or CPC (all) accelerates, ease off the increase. This is a scale-up test, not a cut.

  2. Treat the mild cost inflation as variance — monitor, don't react

    Evidence: CPM +4.2%, CPC (all) +8.6%, CTR (all) −4.0%, against a ~60% spend jump in a single week; Link Click CTR was flat, so intent-bearing clicks held while broader all-clicks engagement softened. Hypothesis: this is normal fluctuation from rapid scaling, not a fatigue trend — one week of aggregate data cannot confirm a creative problem. Action / expected impact: keep monitoring; only test a creative refresh if CTR (all) keeps sliding across several consecutive weeks. Framed as an observation, not a directive to change anything now.

  3. Confirm downstream measurement before optimizing for outcomes

    Evidence: the campaign optimizes for Link Clicks; it drove 314 Landing Page Views at ~$0.27 each, but the data shows no purchase/ROAS events and only single-digit registration/messaging signals. Hypothesis: if the business goal is leads or sign-ups, verifying pixel/conversion-event tracking — and, once enough events accrue, testing a conversion objective — would let future reports judge cost-per-outcome rather than only traffic, improving overall efficiency over time. Caveat: this digest's aggregate data cannot confirm the current tracking state, so treat this as a measurement check to run, not a confirmed gap.

Methodology

This is a weekly executive digest built from aggregate account- and campaign-level totals for the reporting window and the immediately preceding equal-length window (pulled from the Meta Graph API, v21.0). It deliberately reports what changed week over week — not a deep root-cause diagnosis. The data contains no day-by-day time series, no placement/audience breakdowns, and no ad-set or ad-level detail, so mechanisms that require those views (marginal-CPA / Breakdown Effect, learning-phase status, auction-overlap, relevance rankings) are intentionally not asserted here. All figures come directly from the returned data; none are estimated. Money is shown in USD. Audience size is reported as Accounts Center accounts.
