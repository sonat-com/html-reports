Sonat Ad Account

Reporting period: Jun 6–12, 2026  ·  vs. prior week May 30 – Jun 5, 2026  ·  Currency: USD

Executive summary

A leaner, sharper week. Amount Spent eased to $54.04 (−14.7% WoW) while delivery contracted faster than budget — Impressions −28.2% and Reach −29.2% — pushing CPM up 18.8% to $6.06. Despite reaching a smaller audience, the audience that did see the ads engaged noticeably more: CTR (all) rose 30.2% and Link Click CTR rose 20.2%, holding CPC efficiency flat-to-better.

Net effect: fewer impressions but higher-quality clicks. CPC (all) improved to $0.12 (−8.8%) and CPC (Link Click) held at $0.20 (−1.2%), even as each thousand impressions cost more. The single campaign in the account — a top-of-funnel link-clicks effort — is delivering more efficient traffic on a smaller footprint. Conversion outcomes remain minimal at this funnel stage (1 Complete Registration, as in the prior week); purchases/ROAS are not tracked under this objective.

Recommendations — testable hypotheses, not directives

  • Hypothesis · Delivery & CPM

    Diagnose the reach contraction before adjusting budget

    Impressions fell 28.2% and Reach 29.2% while spend fell only 14.7%, so CPM climbed 18.8% — each impression got more expensive, and budget alone doesn't explain the volume drop. Hypothesis: narrower delivery or stronger auction competition reduced the audience served. Test: next week pull a daily delivery and placement breakdown to see whether the CPM rise is broad or concentrated. Do not cut spend on the higher CPM — click efficiency improved at the same time, so a higher average cost here is not evidence of poor performance.

  • Hypothesis · Scale the efficient traffic

    Hold creative and probe a modest budget increase

    CTR (all) rose 30.2% and Link Click CTR rose 20.2% with CPC (Link Click) essentially flat at $0.20 — the current creative is resonating more strongly even on a smaller audience. Hypothesis: efficient link-click volume can scale before fatigue, given Frequency is low (1.14). Test: keep the winning creative steady and trial a small, incremental budget lift, watching CPM and Frequency for the first sign of diminishing efficiency. Expected impact: more efficient link clicks for the account overall, at controlled risk.

  • Hypothesis · Measurement

    Confirm the downstream conversion signal is firing

    The week produced just 1 Complete Registration and the campaign optimizes for link clicks, so there is little conversion signal to learn from. Hypothesis: registration/pixel events may be under-counted, or the objective under-leverages a real downstream goal. Test: verify the registration pixel fires reliably and let events accumulate; only once volume is sufficient consider evaluating a conversion-objective test against the current link-clicks setup. With ~1 registration/week the data cannot yet justify an objective change — this is about building measurement first.

The account's official recommendations feed was empty for this window, so there were no Meta-issued suggestions to surface or defer to. The hypotheses above are deliberately cautious given the high-level, aggregate-only data.

Methodology. This is a weekly executive digest built from aggregate account- and campaign-level totals for Jun 6–12, 2026 versus the preceding equal-length window (May 30 – Jun 5, 2026), pulled from the Meta Graph API (v21.0). It reports what changed week over week. It deliberately does not attempt deep root-cause diagnosis — there is no daily time series, no placement/audience breakdown, and no ad-set or ad-level data in this pull, so mechanisms such as marginal-CPA/Breakdown-Effect, learning-phase status, or auction overlap cannot be substantiated and are not asserted. Reach is reported as Accounts Center accounts; "clicks" is always disambiguated as Clicks (all) vs Link Clicks. All figures come directly from the API response; none are estimated.
