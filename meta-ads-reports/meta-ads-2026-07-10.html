An efficient traffic week. Amount Spent rose a modest +4.1% ($77.55 → $80.71), yet delivery expanded far more: Impressions +13.7% and Reach +18.5%. The driver was cost — CPM fell 8.4% ($6.51 → $5.96), so each dollar bought materially more delivery than the week prior.

Traffic outcomes strengthened in step. Link Clicks grew +11.3% (398 → 443) while CPC (Link Click) fell −6.5% ($0.195 → $0.182), and Landing Page Views climbed +26.2% (282 → 356). CTR (all) held essentially flat (+0.5%); Link-click CTR eased −2.1% as Reach grew slightly faster than Link Clicks. Frequency stayed low at 1.12 (−4.1%), consistent with the campaign's top-of-funnel intent.

This campaign runs a Link Clicks objective, so no Purchase, Purchase ROAS, or conversion metrics are tracked; results are read on cost and traffic efficiency. Net: cheaper delivery, cheaper clicks, and more site visits for a nearly flat budget.

Recommendations

The account returned no official Meta recommendations this window (empty recommendations). The hypotheses below are cautious and framed as tests, consistent with the aggregate, single-window data available.

1. Test a measured budget increase while efficiency is favorable

Evidence: A +4.1% spend increase coincided with CPM −8.4%, CPC (Link Click) −6.5%, and Link Clicks +11.3% — delivery is currently cheap and Frequency is low (1.12), so the audience is not saturated.

Hypothesis / expected impact: A gradual +15–20% budget lift may capture additional Link Clicks near the current cost-per-click rather than bidding into more expensive inventory, improving overall traffic volume at similar efficiency. Scale in steps and watch CPM and CPC (Link Click) for the point where marginal cost starts to rise; hold if they climb.

2. Keep an eye on Link-click CTR even as clicks grow

Evidence: Reach grew +18.5% while Link Clicks grew +11.3%, so Link-click CTR eased −2.1% (3.34% → 3.27%). Volume is up, but each incremental impression converted to a link click slightly less often.

Hypothesis / expected impact: As delivery broadens, refreshing or rotating creative could defend click-through rate and keep CPC (Link Click) low. Treat as a test — measure Link-click CTR before and after — not a directive; CTR (all) is flat, so there is no urgent fatigue signal yet.

3. Confirm conversion tracking matches the funnel intent

Evidence: No Purchase, Purchase ROAS, or sustained lead signal is present this window (Link Clicks objective; incidental leads moved 2 → 0, too small to interpret). Landing Page Views rose +26.2%, so on-site traffic is genuine.

Hypothesis / expected impact: If this TOFU campaign is meant to seed a downstream conversion goal, verifying pixel/conversion events would let future reports judge outcome quality, not just traffic. This is a measurement improvement, not a spend change.
